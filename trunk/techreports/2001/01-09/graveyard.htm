 1: Student request

It is March of 2001, and Melissa Chow, a junior who is majoring in ICS, has reviewed the courses she wishes to take for next Fall and is ready to make her requests to the ICS CourseApproval system.  She goes to the CourseApp website and the Login Page appears. (Note: the pages presented in this and the following scenarios are non-working mockups without any formatting elegance. They are intended to simply illustrate the functional aspects of the application.)

Melissa has never used the CourseApp system before, so she follows the link on the Login Page to the Registration Page.   There, she enters her first name, last name, student ID  (which in her case is her social security number), and her UHUnix account name, then presses the submit button.  A few seconds later, an 8 character password is emailed to her UHUnix account. 

Melissa returns to the Login page and enters her student ID and her new CourseApp password.  Melissa is rewarded with the Main Page.  She clicks on the link to review her previous ICS courses and retrieves the Review Page. She notes that all of the information there is correct.

Next, she clicks on the link to the Request Page. This page allows her to specify the courses she wants to request for Fall, 2001, and her priorities for the classes.  Melissa decides to enroll in ICS 413, 464, and 491.  ICS 491 is her first priority, 464 is second, and 413 is third.  After she submits her request, she is automatically taken to the Submission Page, which provides her with her current course requests and their priorities. 

Seeing that everything is in order, Melissa logs out and waits for the email notification after April 15th of which courses she can enroll in and their approval codes. 

Usage scenario 2: Rule application and administrative review

Finally, April 15 arrives, and it's time to decide upon course approvals. Lei Wakayama, our long-suffering ICS course administrator, logs into the system.  Her account and password indicate that she has administrative privileges on the system, and so after logging in she is taken to the Administrator Main Page. 

Once there, she invokes the first menu item to generate priority values and preliminary course assignments, given the current set of course requests.  Upon clicking on that link, the system chugs away for a few seconds, then takes her to a page containing the Course Assignment Report. 

After reviewing the report, she next decides to check the results by going to the Course Assignments By Student page.  This page contains a table with one line for each of the hundreds of students who have requested ICS courses.  Each line indicates the courses requested by the student; if the course is listed in green, the course request was approved; if the course is listed in red; the course request was denied.  

Lei scans this table looking for any strange entries.  For Lei, that means a situation where a student is denied most or all of their requests who should under normal circumstances get at least one of their requests. The first student who falls into this category is Bill Choe, an upperclassman ICS major who requested and was denied all three of the ICS courses he requested. Lei decides to look at this situation in more detail, presses the "Details" button for Bill Choe, and obtains Bill's Details Page.

The Details page lists information about Bill's prior courses, as well as a listing of his current requests.  If a request had been approved by the system, the checkbox would be checked. If Lei checks a box (or unchecks a checked box, then hits the "Update approvals" button, the system will update the course approvals for that student.

The "Rules" column contains a set of buttons called "Trace". These buttons enable Lei to display the results of invoking each of the rules in order to understand the priority level that the student obtained. Lei hits the trace button for Bill's top priority course and obtains Bill's 413 Trace page. Lei sees from the trace of rule application that Bill's low GPA and relatively few prior courses relative to other students requesting these courses has led to his denial.  

However, she decides to allow Bill entry into one of the requested courses, ICS 413. She does this by going back to Bill's Details Page, checking the checkbox next to 413, and pressing the "Update Approvals" button. 

Lei continues on through the list of students, reviewing those who have been denied excessive courses, and in some cases deciding to overrule the system and grant a request.  

After an hour or so, Lei finishes reviewing the list of course approvals.  She goes back to her main page and presses the Send Email link. This results in the generation of emails to all thousand or so students who requested courses to indicate the results of the approval process.  

Usage scenario 3: Updating the student course information

Once per semester, before the system is opened to students to make requests, the system must be updated with the latest information on courses taken by students and their grades. This information is obtained from ISIS.  The proposed approach is to employ a modified version of Wes's ISIS program to produce an XML version of the data. This XML file is then read into the system using the command on the Administrator's Main page. The program reads in the data associated with each student, and updates their records appropriately.
