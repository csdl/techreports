Collaborative Authoring

AEN supports two methods for collaborative authoring:proofreading & trading the lock. In the proofreading method the author can create a node and ``publish'' it by changing the read and annotate access for the document. Then the other co-authors can make comments about the document. The co-author can make simple as proofreading comments or complex philosophical discussion comments. The author can then read the comments and make changes to the document or comment on the comments. This can lead to discussions on the contents of the document. These comments could be though of as marginal notes. They could be suggested changes, new ideas to include, or just general comments on the subject.

In the trading the lock method the author can create a node and allow other authors to edit the node by giving them write access to the node. When a node is saved all connected users that are viewing the node see the changes. In this way two or more authors could trade the lock and make changes that all the authors will see.
